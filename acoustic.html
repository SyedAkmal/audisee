Acoustics

A home theater can be a considerable investment, and yet expensive electronics alone cannot deliver a great movie experience. The acoustics of a room is a vital component of a home theater system. In fact, an excellent system in an untreated room will have poorer sound quality than an average system in a treated room. We agree with acoustic experts who say, the speaker system and electronics contribute only 40 percent to your system’s overall sonics—with the room being responsible for the other 60 percent. We design and customize acoustic solutions so that neither the systems performance nor the aesthetics of your room are affected.

.

ABSORBERS

Installation of absorptive treatments in the right locations can be dramatically beneficial to a home theater. Proper absorption of the unwanted frequencies will improve the clarity and focus in the sound field and absorbers also help in reducing the reverb time of the room resulting in cinema theater like sound.

BASS TRAP

They absorb renegade bass frequencies that would otherwise bounce around your home theater and make the overall sound quality muddy. Bass traps are acoustic energy absorbers which are designed to damp low frequency sound energy and attain a flatter low frequency (LF) room response, resulting in clean, tight and consistent bass that you love.

DIFUSERS

Most people only hear about "absorption" requirements for a home theater, but a properly treated home theatre room will contain both elements to allow for a more immersive movie watching experience. Diffusion causes sounds to uniformly bounce around the room putting you in the center of the movie. Diffusers improve envelopment using the surround field to create a more immersive experience for the listener.
